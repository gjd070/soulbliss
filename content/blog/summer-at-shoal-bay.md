There's a particular kind of morning you only get in Shoal Bay in summer. The air is warm before 7am, the bay is flat and turquoise, and if you walk down to the water early enough, you'll almost certainly see dolphins cruising the shallows.

Summer runs from December through February, and it's peak season for good reason. Here's what to expect and how to make the most of it.

## The Beach

Shoal Bay is the most sheltered beach in Port Stephens, which makes it ideal for families and swimmers. The water is calm and clear, the sand is wide and white, and the beach is patrolled during summer by Surf Life Saving NSW.

The best time to arrive is early — by 9am the carpark fills quickly in December and January. Walk down from Soul Bliss takes about five minutes and you'll arrive before the crowds.

## Wildlife

Port Stephens is home to over 90 resident bottlenose dolphins, and summer is one of the best times to see them. They move in pods through the bay in the early mornings, often close to shore. Whale watching season technically ends in November, but late humpbacks are sometimes spotted through December.

Keep an eye out from the upper deck — you can often spot dolphins from the house without leaving.

## Tomaree Head

The Tomaree Head summit walk (about 1 hour return) is best done in the early morning before the heat sets in. The views from the top are extraordinary — the full sweep of Port Stephens to the north, Shark Bay below, and on clear days you can see back to Newcastle.

Wear good shoes, bring water, and start before 8am in January and February.

## Evenings

Summer evenings are made for the deck. The sun sets behind Tomaree and the bay turns gold, then pink, then deep blue. The water stays warm enough for a late swim, and Nelson Bay's restaurants and seafood co-op are just ten minutes away.

Book restaurants early in January — they fill fast.
