Shoal Bay sits at the entrance to Tomaree National Park, which means some of the best walking tracks in the Hunter region start almost at the front door. Here are our favourites, from a short beach stroll to a half-day adventure.

## Tomaree Head Summit Walk

**Distance:** 2km return | **Time:** 45–60 min | **Difficulty:** Moderate

This is the walk. Starting from the carpark at the end of Shoal Bay Road, the trail climbs steadily through banksia and scribbly gum to the trig point at the top. The views reward every step — north over Port Stephens, south towards Stockton Bight, and on clear days, all the way to Newcastle.

Go early. The summit is best before 9am when the light is soft and the chances of seeing dolphins in the bay below are highest.

## Shoal Bay to Zenith Beach

**Distance:** 4km return | **Time:** 1–1.5 hours | **Difficulty:** Easy–Moderate

From the southern end of Shoal Bay beach, a track winds around the headland to Zenith Beach — one of the more beautiful and secluded surf beaches in the region. The track has some rocky sections and a short steep section near the headland, but it's manageable for most people.

Zenith is a surf beach — not patrolled — but it's a stunning spot for a sit and a snack before heading back.

## Box Beach Track

**Distance:** 5km return | **Time:** 1.5–2 hours | **Difficulty:** Moderate

Continue past Zenith and you reach Box Beach, another wild and rarely crowded stretch of coast backed by national park. This is a proper bushwalk with undulating terrain and occasional ocean views through the trees.

Bring plenty of water — there are no facilities once you leave Shoal Bay.

## Foreshore to Nelson Bay

**Distance:** 8km one way | **Time:** 2–2.5 hours | **Difficulty:** Easy

For something flat and social, the foreshore path connects Shoal Bay all the way to Nelson Bay along the waterfront. It passes Fingal Bay, the Port Stephens ferry terminal, and arrives at the marina precinct in Nelson Bay where you can reward yourself with lunch.

You can catch a taxi or rideshare back to Soul Bliss, or turn around and do it as a 16km round trip.

## Tips for All Walks

- Carry at least 1 litre of water per person in summer
- A hat and sunscreen are essential year-round
- The national park tracks are mostly unmarked — download the NSW National Parks app before you go
- Dogs are not permitted in Tomaree National Park
