Every year from May to November, humpback whales migrate along the New South Wales coast — and Port Stephens sits right in their path. Thousands of whales make this journey each year, and from the right vantage point, you can watch them pass without leaving the area.

## The Season

Humpbacks migrate north from May through August, heading to warmer Queensland waters. They return south from September through November, often with calves born during the winter. The best sightings tend to happen in June, July, and October — but whales can surprise you any time in the season.

## Where to Watch From Shore

**Tomaree Head** is the best land-based vantage point in the region. The summit sits about 160 metres above sea level and offers an unobstructed view across the mouth of Port Stephens. Early morning is best — the light is good and the sea is usually calmer.

**Shoal Bay foreshore** gives you a low, wide view over the bay. When whales enter the heads, they're sometimes visible from the beach itself.

## Whale Watching Cruises

Several operators run dedicated whale watching cruises from Nelson Bay Marina, about ten minutes from Soul Bliss. These typically run 2–3 hours and get you much closer to the action. Booking ahead is essential in July and August.

**Moonshadow Cruises** and **Imagine Cruises** are two of the most established operators. Both offer guaranteed sightings or a free return trip.

## What You'll See

Humpbacks are acrobatic. Breaching (launching fully out of the water), pec slapping, and tail-lobbing are all common. You may also see them spy-hopping — poking their heads vertically out of the water to have a look around.

Port Stephens is also home to bottlenose dolphins year-round, and you're likely to see them on any cruise or early morning beach walk.

## Tips

- Bring binoculars even if you're doing a shore-based watch
- Morning light makes photography much easier
- June and October tend to have calmer seas than July–August
- Dress in layers — it can be cold offshore even on warm days
